On-campus Resources

Where to self-study? How to get involved?

Study Spaces

Need a quiet place to hit the books? Check out the SCC Libraries for study rooms, computer access, and academic resources. Reserve a group study room online or just drop in for a quiet spot.

The first floor of the SAM Building is also a popular place to study, meet friends, or grab a snack between classes.

Other options include the cafeteria, open lounges in the Broadway Edison building, and outdoor seating areas when the weather is nice.

Getting Involved

Want to meet new people? Join a club! The Student Clubs page lists organizations for all interests—cultural, academic, hobby, and more. If you don’t see a club you like, you can even start your own!

Want to stay updated on campus events? Follow @seattlecollegesintl on Instagram for announcements about workshops, job opportunities, cultural events, volunteer opportunities and more!

✅ Getting involved is a great way to make friends, practice English, and build your resume!

Technology & Printing

Need to print an assignment or borrow a laptop? The library and computer labs offer printing, scanning, and short-term laptop loans. Ask at the front desk for details.

Wi-Fi is available throughout campus—use your student credentials to log in.
